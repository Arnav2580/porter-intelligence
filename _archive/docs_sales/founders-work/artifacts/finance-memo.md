# Finance Memo — Porter Intelligence Platform

Purpose: CFO-ready summary that ties the purchase price to a leakage-reduction investment thesis.

---

## Executive Summary

Porter Intelligence Platform should be evaluated as a leakage-control asset, not as a software purchase.

Porter reported Rs 4,306 crore in revenue for FY25, with Rs 55 crore in net profit — the company's first profitable year. Industry estimates place fraud and operational leakage in intra-city logistics at 2-4% of transaction volume. At Porter's scale, even a 2% leakage rate implies Rs 86 crore in annual loss from fake trips, route manipulation, payout anomalies, cash extortion, and cancellation abuse.

The platform costs Rs 3.25 crore — 0.075% of annual revenue. It needs to recover less than 4% of the conservative leakage estimate to break even in year one.

| Tranche | Amount | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Tranche 1 | Rs 1.00 Cr | Signing — source access, deployment, documentation |
| Tranche 2 | Rs 1.00 Cr | Shadow-mode validation success (60 days) |
| Tranche 3 | Rs 1.25 Cr | Live rollout and handover completion (90 days) |
| **Total** | **Rs 3.25 Cr** | (+ 18% GST) |

Porter only pays the full amount after the platform has proven value on Porter's own data. Tranche 2 and 3 are milestone-gated.

---

## How Leakage Reduction Creates Savings

1. **Direct payout prevention**: fraudulent drivers who are identified and suspended no longer generate fake trip payouts. At Rs 400/trip average, even 5 fake trips/week per fraudulent driver is Rs 2,000/week saved per driver caught.

2. **Payout anomaly correction**: overcharging, fare manipulation, and route padding inflate the payout line item. Each corrected anomaly flows directly to EBITDA.

3. **Reduced investigation cost**: structured analyst queue with pre-assembled evidence reduces average case investigation time from 45+ minutes to 10-15 minutes.

4. **Deterrence effect**: when drivers know that fraud patterns are actively detected and acted upon, the attempt rate drops. This is the long-term multiplier.

---

## Why The Price Is Rational

- Rs 3.25 crore is less than one week of recoverable leakage under the realistic scenario
- An equivalent internal build would cost Rs 8-15 crore in loaded engineering salaries over 12-18 months
- The milestone-gated structure means Porter pays the full amount only after proven value
- No recurring license fee; ongoing cost after handover is infrastructure only (Rs 3-5 lakh/month)

---

## The Buyer-Safe Framing

- Benchmark evidence (synthetic data) is pre-integration evidence — it shows the system works, not that it catches your specific fraud
- Reviewed-case metrics become the buyer-safe truth layer after Porter's analysts validate cases in shadow mode
- Shadow mode reduces rollout risk before any live writeback is considered
- Acceptance criteria are measurable: 70%+ action-tier precision, 95%+ ingestion success, 0 enforcement webhooks during shadow period

---

## Closing Statement

If Porter believes that structured detection and faster intervention can create measurable recoverable value — even at a fraction of the conservative estimate — then this purchase should be evaluated as a capital-allocation decision, not as a line-item software expense.
